# Business

Money, legal, sustainability. The gap between a shipped product and a sustainable business. AI changes the economics: variable inference costs, margins structurally lower than traditional SaaS, and LLM pricing deflating at 10x annually.

## Revenue Design

Pricing strategy, monetization model, and the mechanics of getting paid. In a world where AI changes cost structures for everyone, pricing is the highest-leverage decision a founder makes — entry price anchors perception, and the wrong model locks you into margins you can't escape.

- **Level 0:** No pricing strategy. Product is free with no plan to charge, or price was picked arbitrarily.
- **Level 1:** Has a price but it's based on gut feel or competitor imitation, not cost structure or value delivered. Doesn't account for variable AI costs (inference, API calls) that scale with usage. Hasn't tested pricing with real users.
- **Level 2:** Has a deliberate pricing model that accounts for variable AI costs, tested with real users, with rationale for the choice. Understands the tradeoffs between subscription, usage-based, credit-based, and hybrid models. Price reflects value delivered, not just cost incurred.
- **Level 3:** Pricing is strategic: metering metrics tracked from day one, usage transparency for customers, model designed so revenue scales with the value customers get (not inversely). Understands outcome-based pricing and when it applies. Has raised prices at least once based on evidence. Monitors the "OpenAI test" — would the product survive if the underlying AI provider shipped the feature for free?
- **Level 4:** Pricing frameworks others adopt. Designs monetization strategies that balance growth, margins, and customer trust. Track record across products or advising others.
- **Level 5:** Advances how the field thinks about pricing AI products.

## Unit Economics

CAC, LTV, churn, margins. Understanding whether growth is sustainable or burning cash. AI products have structurally different economics — inference costs as COGS means margins start at 25-40%, not 75-90%.

- **Level 0:** No awareness of unit economics. Doesn't know what it costs to acquire or serve a customer. Revenue feels good but might be unprofitable per user.
- **Level 1:** Tracks revenue but not the costs behind it. Doesn't know CAC, LTV, or churn with precision. Unaware that AI inference costs can consume 15-40% of revenue, making gross margins fundamentally different from traditional SaaS.
- **Level 2:** Tracks cost-per-user and gross margin. Knows churn rate. Can explain whether unit economics improve or degrade with growth. Understands the AI margin challenge — traditional SaaS runs 75-90% gross margin, AI-first products typically run 50-65% at maturity.
- **Level 3:** Unit economics are actively managed: LTV:CAC ratio tracked (targeting 3:1+), cohort-level economics analyzed, cost optimization stack in place (prompt engineering, caching, model routing — combined savings of up to 80%). Knows which customer segments are profitable and which aren't. Churn under 3% monthly with a plan to improve.
- **Level 4:** Unit economics frameworks others adopt. Designs cost structures that scale efficiently. Track record of turning unprofitable AI products profitable.
- **Level 5:** Advances how the field thinks about AI product economics.

## Legal & Compliance

Terms, privacy policy, IP ownership, and AI-specific regulation. The regulatory surface area is expanding fast — EU AI Act enforcement begins August 2026, U.S. states are passing patchwork laws, and AI copyright remains unsettled.

- **Level 0:** No legal documentation. No terms of service, no privacy policy. Unaware of regulatory requirements. Ships AI features without disclosure.
- **Level 1:** Knows legal basics matter but hasn't acted. Might have a template privacy policy. Doesn't know which regulations apply to their product. Unaware that AI-generated content may not be copyrightable or that AI tools can introduce GPL-licensed code.
- **Level 2:** Has terms of service, privacy policy, and AI disclosure where required. Understands which regulations apply (EU AI Act risk tier, relevant state laws) and has made deliberate data handling choices. Knows the basics: human authorship required for copyright, AI training data has legal exposure, user data has deletion obligations.
- **Level 3:** Compliance is proactive: impact assessments for AI features, training data provenance documented, bias testing where required, privacy practices that satisfy GDPR/CCPA. Monitors regulatory changes quarterly. Understands the distinction between what requires legal counsel (hiring AI, health data, multi-state operations) and what can be self-managed (basic chatbots, internal tools, single-state operation).
- **Level 4:** Legal and compliance practices others adopt. Helps founders navigate AI regulation. Designs compliance approaches that don't paralyze shipping speed.
- **Level 5:** Advances how the community thinks about legal and regulatory strategy for AI products.

## Financial Management

Modeling costs, managing runway, and making sound investment decisions. AI businesses have a unique cost profile: inference costs are variable COGS that scale with usage, making cash flow less predictable than traditional software.

- **Level 0:** No financial tracking beyond checking the bank balance. Doesn't know monthly burn rate. No separation of fixed and variable costs.
- **Level 1:** Tracks revenue and total expenses but doesn't separate fixed from variable costs. Doesn't model how costs change with growth. LLM spending is unmonitored — no per-user or per-feature cost visibility.
- **Level 2:** Knows monthly costs with fixed/variable split, especially inference spend. Has basic LLM cost optimization in place (model selection, caching, prompt efficiency). Revenue path exceeds burn rate. Can calculate runway under current and growth scenarios.
- **Level 3:** Financial management is deliberate: cost tracking per feature and per customer segment, model routing optimizing cost-quality tradeoffs (80% cheap models, 20% frontier), batch processing for async work. Maintains 24+ months runway or is profitable. Has chosen the right business structure for their revenue level. Treats financial health as a weekly check, not a quarterly surprise.
- **Level 4:** Financial management practices others adopt. Designs cost structures and optimization strategies for AI businesses. Advises other founders on financial sustainability.
- **Level 5:** Advances how the field thinks about financial management for AI-first businesses.
